Nelson Mandela

1918 - 2013

“Do not judge me by my successes, judge me by
how many times I fell down and got back up again.”

The First Black President of South Africa.

Nelson Mandela (1918-2013) was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and politician. He co-founded the ANC Youth League and led peaceful protests against racial discrimination. Imprisoned for 27 years, he became a global symbol of resistance. After release, he negotiated apartheid's end and became South Africa's first black president in 1994. Mandela focused on reconciliation and social reforms during his presidency, leaving a legacy of forgiveness and unity.

Biography Of Nelson Mandela-

  • Born on July 18, 1918, in Mvezo, Transkei, South Africa.
  • Attended Fort Hare University and later studied law at the University of Witwatersrand.
  • Joined the African National Congress (ANC) in the 1940s, advocating for an end to apartheid.
  • Co-founded the ANC Youth League in 1944, promoting nonviolent resistance against racial discrimination.
  • Became a prominent anti-apartheid activist, leading peaceful protests and advocating for civil rights.
  • In 1961, he co-founded Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK), the armed wing of the ANC, to resist apartheid with acts of sabotage.
  • Arrested in 1962 and sentenced to life imprisonment in 1964 during the Rivonia Trial.
  • Spent 27 years in prison on Robben Island, Pollsmoor Prison, and Victor Verster Prison.
  • Gained international recognition as a symbol of the anti-apartheid struggle.
  • Released from prison on February 11, 1990, following global pressure and internal reforms.
  • Played a key role in negotiations to end apartheid, leading to South Africa's first democratic elections in 1994.
  • Elected as South Africa's first black president in the historic 1994 elections.
  • Initiated policies aimed at reconciliation and dismantling apartheid's legacy.
  • Established the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC) to address past human rights abuses.
  • Served as President from 1994 to 1999, focusing on social and economic reforms.
  • Stepped down as President in 1999, declining a second term.
  • Advocated for human rights, education, and HIV/AIDS awareness after leaving office.
  • Received numerous awards, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.
  • Passed away on December 5, 2013, at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of forgiveness and unity.
